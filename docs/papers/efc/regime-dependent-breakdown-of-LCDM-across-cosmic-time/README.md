## Abstract

Multiple observational tensions with LCDM have been reported across disparate redshifts. We present a systematic analysis combining low-redshift galaxy dynamics (SPARC, z ~ 0) with high-redshift galaxy abundances (COSMOS-Web/JWST, z > 6) to test whether deviations from standard predictions cluster in identifiable regimes.

**Key result:** Deviations cluster systematically (chi-squared = 47.3, df = 4, p < 10^-8), establishing structured regime dependence without proposing alternative physics.

---

## Key Findings

### Statistical Significance
- **chi-squared:** 47.3
- **Degrees of freedom:** 4
- **p-value:** 1.3 x 10^-9
- **Confidence:** >99.99%

### High-Redshift Excess (JWST/COSMOS2025)

| Redshift | Ratio vs Behroozi | Ratio vs Halo Limit |
|----------|-------------------|---------------------|
| z = 5-6  | 23x               | 3.1x                |
| z = 7-8  | 124x              | 7.7x                |
| z = 8-9  | 259x              | 10.4x               |
| z = 9-10 | 529x              | 10.6x               |

## What This Paper Does NOT Claim

1. **Does NOT falsify LCDM** - regime structure may be accommodated within standard framework
2. **Does NOT propose alternative dynamics** - no modified gravity or new physics invoked
3. **Does NOT identify unique mechanism** - multiple explanations remain viable
