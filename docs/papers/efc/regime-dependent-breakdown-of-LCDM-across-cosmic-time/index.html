Regime-Dependent Breakdown of LCDM Across Cosmic Time

Morten Magnusson | Energy-Flow Cosmology Initiative

January 2026 ORCID DOI: 10.6084/m9.figshare.31061665

Abstract

Multiple observational tensions with LCDM have been reported across disparate redshifts. We present a systematic analysis combining low-redshift galaxy dynamics (SPARC, z ~ 0) with high-redshift galaxy abundances (COSMOS-Web/JWST, z > 6) to test whether deviations from standard predictions cluster in identifiable regimes. Using a descriptive taxonomy (L0-L3), we find that deviations are not uniformly distributed but cluster systematically.

Key Result: chi-squared = 47.3, df = 4, p = 1.3 x 10^-9
Rejects uniform distribution at >99.99% confidence.

Scope of Claims

What We Show

  • Deviations cluster systematically by regime
  • Pattern observed at both low-z and high-z
  • Statistical significance: p < 10^-8

What We Do NOT Claim

  • Does NOT falsify LCDM
  • Does NOT propose alternative dynamics
  • Does NOT identify unique mechanism
